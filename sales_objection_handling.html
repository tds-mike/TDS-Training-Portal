Diffusing Objections with NEPQ

The Mindset for Objections

An "objection" is not a rejection—it's a sign of engagement. It's a request for more information, a test of our value, or a concern that needs reassurance. Our job is not to "handle" or "fight" objections, but to welcome them as an opportunity to understand the customer better and guide them to the right solution. A confident, empathetic approach is key.

The 3-Step Formula for Diffusing Concerns

1

Clarify

Understand what their concern *really* is. Paraphrase their concern to show you're listening (e.g., "So it sounds like the main concern is the upfront investment?"). Don't assume you know.

2

Discuss

Talk about the concern conversationally. Use analogies to re-frame value (e.g., "It's like a screen protector for your phone, but for your car's paint.").

3

Diffuse

Ask questions that help them overcome their own concern. Use the "Direct Response" questions to guide them back to the value they're seeking.

A Library of Common Objections & Responses

Concern: "I'll call you back / I'll let you know."

Step 1: Clarify

"Of course. Just so I know what information would be most helpful for you when you're thinking it over, what's the biggest question on your mind right now?"

Step 2: Discuss & Diffuse

This objection is often a polite way of hiding the real concern (usually price or needing to speak with a spouse). Your job is to gently uncover it. Once they state the real concern, you can address it directly using the appropriate script below.

Concern: "It's too expensive."

Step 1: Clarify

"I understand. When you say it's expensive, are you thinking about the upfront cost, or the long-term value?"

Step 2: Discuss & Diffuse

"I agree it's a significant investment, but let's compare it to the alternative. The cost to repaint a bumper from rock chip damage is often over $1,000. This PPF package protects that investment for years and preserves your resale value. To make it more manageable, we do have financing options available. Would breaking it down into smaller monthly payments be helpful?"

Concern: "Is a ceramic coating really worth it compared to just waxing?"

Step 1: Clarify

"That's a great question. It really comes down to what you value more: short-term shine or long-term protection and ease of maintenance."

Step 2: Discuss & Diffuse

"Wax is like a temporary glow—it looks great for a few weeks, but you have to reapply it constantly. Our ceramic coating is more like adding an extra layer of clear coat. It's a semi-permanent shield that protects your paint for years, not weeks. Would you agree that making your car much easier to clean for years to come would be a huge benefit?" (Direct Response Question)

Concern: "Will the PPF turn yellow over time?"

Step 1: Clarify

"I'm glad you brought that up. That's a common concern based on older, lower-quality films from years ago."

Step 2: Discuss & Diffuse

"The modern, premium films we use are completely different. They are optically clear and contain UV inhibitors that prevent yellowing. We are so confident in the technology that we back it with a 10-year warranty against yellowing, cracking, or peeling. Does knowing it's protected by a long-term warranty give you peace of mind?"

Role-Playing Scenario: Diffusing "I'll call you back"

Client: "Okay, that all sounds good. Thanks for the information, I'll call you back to schedule."

Salesperson (Clarify): "Of course, take all the time you need. Just so I can make a note, what's the biggest factor you'll be considering when you think it over?"

Client: "Well, it's a bit more than I was expecting to spend."

Salesperson (Discuss): "I completely understand that. It's definitely a premium investment. The main reason it's priced this way is because it's a permanent solution that protects you from a much larger expense, like having to repaint a bumper down the road."

Salesperson (Diffuse): "You mentioned earlier how frustrating it is to see new chips on your car after every highway drive. If we could find a way to make this investment fit your budget with a monthly payment plan, would you agree that getting that peace of mind now is the right move for your new car?"

Client: "A payment plan could work. What would that look like?"
