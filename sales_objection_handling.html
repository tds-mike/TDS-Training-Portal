Diffusing Objections with NEPQ

In the NEPQ framework, an "objection" is not a rejectionâ€”it's a concern. It's a request for more information or reassurance. Our job is not to "handle" objections, but to diffuse them by understanding the real issue behind the words.

The 3-Step Formula for Diffusing Concerns

1

Clarify

Understand what their concern really is, and why they have it. Don't assume.

2

Discuss

Talk about the concern conversationally, as two people on the same team trying to find a solution.

3

Diffuse

Ask questions that help them overcome their own concern in their own mind.

Common Concerns & How to Diffuse Them

Concern: "It's too expensive."

Step 1: Clarify

"When you say it's too expensive, how do you mean exactly?"

Step 2: Discuss & Diffuse

"I understand it's a significant investment. Let's look at it this way: the cost to repaint a bumper from rock chip damage is often over $1,000. This PPF package protects that investment for years. How do you think you can resolve that where you can find the money so that you can protect your new car?"

Concern: "I need to think about it."

Step 1: Clarify

"Absolutely, it's an important decision. To help you think it over, what's the biggest question or concern on your mind right now? Is it about the process, the price, or something else?"

Step 2: Discuss & Diffuse

Once you understand the specific concern (e.g., price), you can address it directly using the appropriate diffusing questions.

Concern: "Can you just send me a quote/information?"

Step 1: Clarify

"Yes, that's not a problem. Just so I can put together the best information for you, what exactly are you looking for?"

Step 2: Discuss & Diffuse

"It might make sense before I send a quote if I understood a little bit more about your situation, just to see if I could even help you in the first place. For example, what type of challenges have you had with your paint...?" (Transition into your NEPQ questions)

Later in the conversation: "Now let's assume for a minute we get you the quote and we were able to meet your needs that you mentioned to me. What do you see as the next step?"
