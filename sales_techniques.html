Mastering the Sales Process SOP

This is the central roadmap for the entire customer journey at The Detail Shop. It outlines the process from the first inquiry to post-service follow-up, integrating our digital tools and communication strategies into a seamless, premium workflow.

Phase 1: The First Impression

The goal is to provide a rapid, professional, and reassuring response that immediately sets us apart from the competition.

Inquiry Handling Protocol (GoHighLevel)

  • Initial Response Time: Inquiries before 2:00 PM are followed up same-day. After 2:00 PM, next morning.
  • Follow-Up Cadence: Adhere to the automated follow-up sequences. Always **call first, then text** if there is no answer. Log all outcomes.

The Urable Proposal: Formalizing the Quote

For every new lead, create a Proposal in Urable. This provides a professional, detailed document outlining services and costs, serving as our primary tool for quoting.

Elevate the Experience: The "What to Expect" Email

After a positive call where an appointment is likely, send a personalized "What to Expect" email. This builds anticipation and reinforces their decision to choose us.

Phase 2: The Onboarding Experience & Authorization

This is where we define the TDS Experience. We transition from salesperson to trusted consultant, demonstrating a level of professionalism and meticulousness that justifies our premium pricing.

The Consultative Walk-Around Checklist

  • Initiate the Job in Urable WITH the Customer:
    • Begin by converting their proposal or creating a new job.
    • Accurately input the vehicle's VIN, mileage, and plate information in front of them. This act shows transparency and meticulousness.
  • Conduct a Thorough Digital Inspection:
    • Using a tablet, walk the vehicle with the customer. Photograph and document every pre-existing issue in the "Inspection" tab of Urableâ€”swirls, scratches, curb rash, interior stains.
    • Get the customer's signature on the inspection to confirm the vehicle's condition upon arrival.
  • Diagnose, Educate, and Upsell (Plant the Seed):
    • Ask the customer about areas of special concern and take detailed notes in the job file.
    • Exterior: Point out swirls, scratches, and water spots. Use a paint depth gauge for coating/correction jobs and explain, "We measure the paint to ensure we can correct it safely." This is your chance to upsell to a necessary paint correction.
    • Interior: Note heavily soiled areas or damage. Explain what is and isn't possible: "We can significantly improve this stain, but some setting may have occurred."
    • Utilize in-shop displays (heat lamp, coated hood) to let them physically experience the difference our services make.

    • The TDS Welcome Packet

    Present the customer with a branded folder containing your business card, relevant CARPRO brochures for their chosen services, and a printed copy of the aftercare instructions. This tangible packet adds a layer of professionalism and ensures they have all necessary information.

  • Set Final Expectations & Review Approvals:
    • Clearly state the full scope of work, the expected outcome ("This will remove ~85% of swirls"), and the exact pick-up time. Ask them if they have any final questions.
    • Verbally review the key points of the Work Authorization and other required approval documents before sending them for signature.

    • The Approval Documents: Non-Negotiable Authorization

    A technician cannot begin work until all documents (Work Authorization, Tint, PPF, or Coating approvals) are digitally signed in Urable. This protects the business and confirms the customer understands the scope of work.

  • Finalize the Booking:
    • Once all documents are signed, take the 50% deposit.
    • Discuss the referral program incentives while processing the payment.
    • Mark the vehicle as "Dropped Off" in Urable.
    • Enter any necessary "Custom Data" at the bottom of the invoice.

Phase 3: Proactive Communication & Upselling

Maintain communication and transparency while the vehicle is in our care to build value and peace of mind. This is also a key opportunity to make expert recommendations for additional services.

Progress Update Protocol

  • Monitor technician progress and photos in Urable. These photos are your most powerful sales tools.

    • The Mid-Service Upsell Opportunity

    When a technician uncovers a condition that could be improved with an additional service, it creates a natural and low-pressure upsell opportunity. Your role is to present this as a professional recommendation based on their expert findings.

    • Paint Correction: Technician notes a Level 1 isn't fully clearing up deeper swirls. You can recommend upgrading to a Level 2.
    • Ceramic Coating: Vehicle is prepped for a paint coating. This is the perfect time to recommend adding a wheel, glass, or interior coating.
    • PPF: While applying a front end kit, you can recommend adding coverage to other high-impact areas like rocker panels or A-pillars.
    • Tint: After tinting the main windows, a sunstrip is an easy and logical add-on.
  • First Update: Send a compelling 50/50 photo to the customer mid-service. This visually demonstrates the value of the work and builds excitement.

    • Second Update: Build Anticipation

    Send a second, brief text when the job is nearing completion. This personal touch shows you're actively managing their project and gets them excited for the pickup.

  • Ensure the job is on track for the promised pick-up time. Communicate any potential delays immediately.

Phase 4: The Final Impression & Long-Term Loyalty

The final handoff is our last chance to solidify the premium TDS Experience, ensuring the customer leaves feeling wowed and valued, turning them into a long-term client and advocate.

The Professional Handoff Checklist

  • Showcase the Transformation: Walk the customer around their vehicle, using the technician's before/after photos on a tablet to highlight the results.
  • Educate on Aftercare: Explain how to maintain the work. Hand them any physical warranty booklets (for coatings/PPF) and recommend maintenance products.
  • Schedule the Next Appointment: Proactively book their first maintenance wash.
  • Finalize the Transaction: Have the customer sign the "Work Completed" approval, then take the final payment.
  • Launch the Referral Program: Hand the customer three physical referral cards and instruct them to write their name on the back.
  • Set the Stage for a Review: Inform them they will receive an automated link from Urable asking for a review.

The Personal Follow-Up

While Urable handles automated follow-ups, a personal touch goes a long way. Make a quick, personal call or send a text 3-7 days after a major service. This is a powerful tool for building loyalty.
