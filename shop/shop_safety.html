Safety: The Foundation of Professionalism

Module 1: Mindset & General Shop Environment

A Culture of Safety

Safety is not a checklist; it's a mindset. A true professional is a safe professional. Every shortcut taken is a risk to yourself, your team, the client's vehicle, and the business. A clean, organized, and aware workspace is the hallmark of a high-level technician. Take pride in maintaining it.

Situational Awareness

  • Trip Hazards: Hoses and cords are the #1 hazard. Always keep them coiled and out of primary walkways. Never leave tools on the floor.
  • Wet Floors: Immediately clean up any spills, no matter how small. A wet floor is a major slip risk.
  • Vehicle Movements: Be aware of vehicles entering or leaving the bays. Never assume a driver sees you.

Chemical Handling & Storage

  • Label Everything: Any chemical transferred from its original container into a secondary container (like a spray bottle) MUST be labeled with the chemical name and its dilution.
  • One Chemical, One Towel: Never use the same towel for different chemicals without washing it first. Cross-contamination can cause damage or create hazardous reactions.
  • Storage: Store all chemicals in their designated cabinets. Flammable liquids must be kept in the fire-rated safety cabinet. Always keep containers sealed.

Module 3: Equipment & Tool Safety

Electrical Tools

  • Inspect Cords: Before plugging anything in, inspect the entire cord for frays or damage. Report damaged equipment immediately.
  • Cord Management: When polishing, always drape the cord over your shoulder to keep it away from the spinning pad.
  • GFCI Outlets: Only use outlets protected by a Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI), especially in the wash bay.

High-Pressure Equipment

  • Pressure Washers: Never point the nozzle at a person. High-pressure injection injuries are severe medical emergencies. Use appropriate nozzles for paint (40-degree white tip).
  • Steamers: High-temperature steam can cause severe burns instantly. Use insulated gloves and be aware of where you are directing the steam nozzle at all times.

Ozone Generator Protocol: CRITICAL DANGER

Ozone is highly effective for odor removal but is TOXIC to humans and pets. It can cause severe respiratory damage.

  1. The machine must only be run in a completely sealed, unoccupied vehicle.
  2. Place a clear warning sign on the driver's side window.
  3. After treatment is complete, open all doors and allow the vehicle to air out for a MINIMUM of 30-60 minutes before anyone is allowed to enter.

Module 4: Personal & Vehicle Safety

Ergonomics & Physical Health

This is a marathon, not a sprint. Protect your body to ensure a long career.

  • Lifting: Lift with your legs, not your back.
  • Repetitive Motion: Vary your tasks. Use a rolling stool or knee pads for low work to reduce strain on your back and knees.
  • Hearing Protection: The combined noise of polishers, vacuums, and air compressors can cause permanent hearing loss over time. Wear hearing protection during prolonged use.

Vehicle Handling Safety

  • Jacks & Stands: If a vehicle must be lifted, always use jack stands. Never work on a vehicle supported only by a jack.
  • Battery Terminals: Be cautious when working in engine bays. Avoid creating a circuit across battery terminals with metal tools.
  • Modern Vehicles: Be aware of keyless entry and auto-locking features. Always keep the client's keys in a designated safe spot to avoid being locked out.
