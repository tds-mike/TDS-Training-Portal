Exterior Mastery: The Foundation

This module covers the foundational skills of all exterior detailing: the wash and decontamination process. Mastering these steps is non-negotiable, as they create the clean canvas required for all other services, from simple waxes to advanced paint correction and ceramic coatings.

The Wash Bay: Foundation of a Flawless Finish

The Crucial Role of Decontamination

A vehicle's paint is constantly bombarded with contaminants. Attempting to polish or protect a contaminated surface is like rubbing sandpaper on the paint, inflicting deeper swirls. A thorough chemical and mechanical decontamination is the non-negotiable first step to any true paint enhancement. It creates a truly clean slate, allowing polishes to work correctly and coatings to bond permanently.

Procedure: The Full Decontamination Wash (Idiot-Proof Guide)

Goal: To remove ALL above and below-surface bonded contaminants. Time: 45-90 minutes.

Phase 1: Diagnosis & Setup

The most critical thinking happens before water touches the car. A correct diagnosis dictates the entire workflow.

  • Visual & Tactile Inspection:
    1. Visual Inspection: Use a swirl finder light to inspect every panel for major defects, heavy water spotting, or bird dropping etches.
    2. Tactile Inspection (Baggie Test): Place your hand inside a thin plastic sandwich bag and gently feel the paint. If it feels gritty or rough, it requires a full decontamination.
  • Make the Soap Decision:
    1. If the paint is covered in visible, crusted-on water spots or the client has known hard water, you will use CARPRO Descale (acidic soap) for the contact wash.
    2. For all other scenarios, you will use CARPRO Reset (pH-neutral soap).
  • Prepare Your Buckets:
    1. Place a grit guard into the bottom of two separate wash buckets.
    2. Fill both buckets about three-quarters full with water.
    3. Designate one bucket as "Rinse" (water only). Add 1-2oz of your chosen soap (Reset or Descale) to the "Wash" bucket.
Phase 2: Meticulous Wheel & Detail Cleaning

We clean the dirtiest parts first to avoid contaminating clean paint. Use dedicated tools for each area.

  • The Wheel, Tire, & Well Deep Clean (One Corner at a Time):
    1. Rinse Liberally: Use the pressure washer to blast away all loose dirt and brake dust from the wheel face, barrel, tire, and the entire plastic fender liner.
    2. Apply Chemicals: Spray CARPRO WheelX on the wheel face and barrel. Spray CARPRO ReTyre on the tire sidewall. Spray a diluted APC (MultiX) inside the fender well. Do not let these chemicals dry.
    3. Agitate Systematically: Use a large, soft barrel brush for the inner wheel barrels. Use a smaller, soft-bristle face brush for the wheel faces and around lug nuts. Use a stiff, dedicated tire brush to scrub the tire sidewall until the foam turns white, indicating old dressing is removed. Use a long-handle, stiff-bristle brush for the fender liner.
    4. Rinse Meticulously: Pressure wash the entire area again, ensuring all chemical residue and grime is completely flushed out. Repeat this entire process for the other three corners.
  • Touchless Pre-Soak & Detail Brush Work:
    1. Foam the entire vehicle with CARPRO Lift.
    2. While the foam is dwelling, use a soft, dedicated detail brush to gently agitate grime in every single intricate area. This includes: around all emblems, inside the grille, the entire gas cap housing, along all window trim and rubber seals, and inside the crevices of headlights and taillights.
    3. Rinse the entire vehicle thoroughly from the top down.
Phase 3: Full Body Decontamination

This multi-step process removes all remaining contaminants, from visible dirt to invisible embedded particles.

  • Two-Bucket Contact Wash & Detail Areas:

    The two-bucket method is essential for preventing scratches during the contact wash.

    1. Submerge your clean wash mitt in the "Wash" bucket to load it with soap.
    2. Wash one small section (e.g., half the roof) following the strict Wash Order (WO): Roof → Glass → Hood/Trunk → Upper Sides → Front/Rear Bumpers → Lower Sides. Use straight, overlapping passes with light pressure.
    3. After each section, immediately dunk the dirty mitt into the "Rinse" bucket and scrub it against the grit guard to release contaminants.
    4. Squeeze out the excess water from the mitt, then return to the "Wash" bucket to pick up fresh soap for the next section.
    5. Lower Panels & Bumpers: For heavily soiled lower rocker panels and bumpers, use a large, soft body brush (dipped in your wash bucket) for more effective cleaning.
    6. Jambs & Sills: Before the final rinse, open doors, trunk, and the gas cap. Use a dedicated mitt or towel with your wash solution to meticulously clean all painted door jambs, trunk sills, and the gas cap area. Rinse these areas gently with low pressure.

    Washing Large Vehicles (Trucks/Vans)

    Use a step ladder to safely reach the center of the roof. A long-handled pole brush with a soft head can be used to effectively clean the upper sections of large vehicles, ensuring no area is missed.

  • Chemical Decontamination (Iron & Tar):

    The dramatic purple reaction shows iron fallout being safely dissolved from the paint.

    1. After the contact wash, rinse the entire car.
    2. While the surface is wet, spray CARPRO IronX liberally over every panel. Wait 2-3 minutes. You will see the chemical react and "bleed" purple as it dissolves iron particles. Do not let it dry.
    3. Rinse the vehicle thoroughly.
    4. Next, spray CARPRO TarX onto a microfiber applicator (NOT directly on the paint) and gently wipe any visible black tar specs, usually found on lower body panels. The tar will dissolve instantly.
  • Mechanical Decontamination (Clay Towel):
    1. Work on one panel at a time (e.g., the hood). The vehicle can be wet or dry.
    2. Generously spray the panel with CARPRO Ech2o as a lubricant. The surface must be very slick.
    3. Gently glide your clay towel over the lubricated surface in straight lines with ZERO pressure. You will feel and hear the contamination being sheared away.
    4. When the towel glides silently and smoothly, the panel is fully decontaminated. Wipe the panel dry with a clean microfiber towel and feel the glass-smooth surface.
    5. Fold the clay towel to a clean side after each major panel.
Phase 4: Final Rinse & Safe Drying

Improper drying can reintroduce scratches. This process ensures a perfect, streak-free, and safe finish.

  • Final Thorough Rinse:
    1. Work from top to bottom, ensuring all chemical and clay lube residue is completely removed from all surfaces and panel gaps.
  • Air Dry Crevices:
    1. Systematically use the air blower to force all trapped water from panel gaps, mirrors, emblems, lights, and grilles. This is critical to prevent drips that will ruin later steps like polishing or coating.
  • Towel Dry Body Panels:

    The drag method minimizes pressure on the paint, reducing the risk of scratches.

    1. Lightly mist one panel at a time with a drying aid to provide lubrication.
    2. The Drag Method: Lay a large, plush, and completely clean drying towel flat on the panel. Gently drag the towel towards you in one direction. Do not apply pressure or swirl the towel in circles.
    3. Pat Dry Remaining Drops: Use a second, smaller plush towel to gently pat dry any remaining water spots or streaks.
Phase 5: Post-Wash Cleanup & Staging

A professional finishes the job completely. This means resetting your workspace for the next vehicle, ensuring efficiency and maintaining tool integrity.

  • Tool & Bucket Cleaning:
    1. Thoroughly rinse all wash mitts, brushes, and bucket grit guards.
    2. Empty and rinse out both wash buckets completely.
    3. Hang mitts and brushes to dry in the designated area.
  • Wash Bay Reset:
    1. Squeegee all excess water from the wash bay floor towards the drain.
    2. Coil all hoses neatly and return the pressure washer gun to its holster.
    3. Return all chemical bottles to their proper storage locations with caps secured.

Procedure: The Basic Maintenance Wash

Goal: To safely clean a well-maintained or ceramic-coated vehicle without inflicting new scratches. Time: 20-45 minutes.

  • Initial Rinse & Wheels: Rinse the entire vehicle to remove loose debris. Perform the standard wheel, tire, and well cleaning procedure.
  • Foam Application: Use a foam cannon to cover the vehicle in a thick layer of pH-neutral soap like CARPRO Reset. This provides essential lubrication.
  • Gentle Two-Bucket Wash: Using a clean wash mitt and the two-bucket method, gently wash the vehicle following the Wash Order (WO). There is no need to scrub; the goal is to gently lift dirt off the protected surface.
  • Thorough Rinse & Dry: Rinse the vehicle completely. Dry using an air blower for crevices and the safe "drag method" with a plush drying towel and a drying aid for the panels.
  • Optional "Wash & Seal": For added efficiency and protection, you can substitute CARPRO Hydro2 Foam in the foam cannon step. After foaming and rinsing, the vehicle will have a fresh layer of SiO2 sealant, creating intense water beading. Skip any separate wax/sealant step if using this method.

Protection: Applying Waxes & Sealants

After decontamination, applying a layer of protection is essential to preserve the finish. Waxes and sealants are excellent entry-level options.

Our Protection Arsenal (Waxes & Sealants)

Wax: Collinite 845

A legendary hybrid wax known for its impressive durability (for a wax), ease of use, and warm, deep carnauba gloss. Excellent for customers seeking a traditional, high-quality wax finish.

Sealant: CARPRO Reload 2.0

Our standard for robust, short-to-mid-term protection. This SiO2 spray sealant offers fantastic gloss, extreme hydrophobicity (water beading), and durability for up to 6 months.

Wash & Seal: CARPRO Hydro2 Foam

An efficient touchless sealant applied via foam cannon during the wash process. Provides several months of SiO2 protection and hydrophobicity. Ideal for maintenance clients.
