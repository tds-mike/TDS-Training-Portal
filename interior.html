Advanced Diagnostics & Strategy

The Goal: Factory Fresh, Not Just Clean

Our objective is to restore an interior to its best possible condition. This means addressing every crack, crevice, and surface. A thorough initial assessment is crucial to identify problem areas, set realistic customer expectations, and formulate an efficient plan.

NOTE: The salesperson is tasked with selling the suitable package and setting cleanliness expectations. This information must be conveyed to the technician before work commences to ensure team alignment.

The Assessment Protocol:

  • Material Identification: This is a critical safety step. Correctly identify materials before applying chemicals.
    • Coated Leather vs. Uncoated: Most modern leather is coated. A drop of water will bead on it. Uncoated leather (high-end luxury cars) absorbs water quickly. Uncoated requires specialized pH-neutral cleaners.
    • Alcantara/Suede: This is a delicate microfiber, not leather. It must be cleaned with a dedicated Alcantara cleaner, never a degreaser.
    • Plastics: Note the difference between hard, durable plastics and soft-touch, easily scratched plastics on dashboards.
  • Problem Area Triage: Locate the worst areas. Specific spills? Heavy staining? This is where you will focus your most intensive efforts.

The TDS Method: Procedures

Procedure: Full Interior Cleaning

This is the comprehensive process to restore a vehicle's interior to the best possible condition. Time: 2-5 hours.

  1. De-Clutter: Begin in the driver's seat and proceed counter-clockwise. Remove all customer items, bag them, and remove large debris.
  2. Air Purge: Use air tools to blow out all hard-to-reach areas (vents, under seats, crevices), working from the back of the vehicle towards the front.
  3. Meticulous Vacuum: Vacuum all surfaces, including seats, carpets, and floor mats, to remove all loose dirt and pet hair.
  4. Deep Clean Surfaces: Starting from the center of the vehicle and working outwards, use appropriate cleaners (e.g., CARPRO Inside) and tools (brushes, steamer) to meticulously clean every surfaceâ€”dash, console, door panels, pillars, and headliner.
  5. Address Stains: Use appropriate spot treatments and techniques on upholstery and carpets. Utilize the steam cleaner and extractor for deep cleaning and stain removal.
  6. Protect Surfaces: Apply interior dressings (e.g., CARPRO PERL) to plastics and vinyl. Condition or coat leather surfaces.
  7. Clean Glass: Clean all interior glass surfaces for a streak-free finish.
  8. Final Inspection: Perform a final vacuum, install any air fresheners, and conduct a thorough quality check to ensure the interior meets TDS standards.

Procedure: Basic Interior Cleaning (Maintenance)

A streamlined process for well-maintained vehicles. Time: 30-60 mins.

  1. Remove large items and debris if necessary.
  2. Blow out the entire vehicle with an air gun, working from back to front.
  3. Thoroughly vacuum all carpets, seats, and mats.
  4. Using CARPRO InnerQD, wipe down and protect all hard surfaces (dash, doors, console).
  5. Clean all interior glass with CARPRO Clarify.
  6. (Optional) Apply CARPRO PERL dressing to leather seats and panels if needed.
