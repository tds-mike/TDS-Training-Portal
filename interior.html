Advanced Diagnostics & Strategy

The Goal: Factory Fresh, Not Just Clean

Our objective is to restore an interior to its best possible condition. This means addressing every crack, crevice, and surface. A thorough initial assessment is crucial to identify problem areas, set realistic customer expectations, and formulate an efficient plan.

NOTE: The salesperson is tasked with selling the suitable package and setting cleanliness expectations. This information must be conveyed to the technician before work commences to ensure team alignment.

The Assessment Protocol:

  • Material Identification: This is a critical safety step. Correctly identify materials before applying chemicals.
    • Coated Leather vs. Uncoated: Most modern leather is coated. A drop of water will bead on it. Uncoated leather (high-end luxury cars) absorbs water quickly. Uncoated requires specialized pH-neutral cleaners.
    • Alcantara/Suede: This is a delicate microfiber, not leather. It must be cleaned with a dedicated Alcantara cleaner, never a degreaser.
    • Plastics: Note the difference between hard, durable plastics and soft-touch, easily scratched plastics on dashboards.
  • Problem Area Triage: Locate the worst areas. Specific spills? Heavy staining? Pet hair? This is where you will focus your most intensive efforts.

The TDS Method: Service Levels & Procedures

Procedure: The Full Interior Restoration Workflow

This is the comprehensive, systematic process to restore an interior to the best possible condition efficiently. Target Time: 1-3 hours.

Phase 1: The Purge (Dry Prep)
  1. Complete De-Clutter: Remove floor mats first. Then, remove all personal items and trash from the entire vehicle and bag them for the customer.
  2. The "Back-to-Front" Air Purge: Start in the trunk/cargo area. Using a Tornador air tool, blow all loose debris from crevices, vents, and under seats, working systematically from the back of the car towards the front doors. This centralizes all dry debris in the front footwells.
  3. The Bulk Vacuum & Pet Hair Removal:
    • For stubborn, embedded pet hair, use a rubber pet hair brush or stone to rake the hair into piles *before* vacuuming. This is far more effective than vacuuming alone.
    • Perform a quick but thorough initial vacuum of the entire vehicle, focusing on the large piles of debris you just created.
Phase 2: The Loop (Deep Cleaning)

Work around the car in a circle to avoid re-contaminating cleaned areas. Always work from top to bottom within each section.

  1. Headliner First: Gently spot-clean the headliner with CARPRO Inside on a microfiber towel. Never spray directly onto the headliner.
  2. Address Spots & Stains: Before general cleaning, treat specific stains on carpets and upholstery. Refer to the "Advanced Stain Removal Guide" for the correct chemical and technique.
  3. The Driver's Side Loop: Clean the driver's door panel, dash, center console, seat, and carpets.
  4. The Rear Loop: Clean the rear driver's side door and seat, then move across to the passenger side rear seat and door.
  5. The Passenger's Side Loop: Clean the passenger's door panel, dash, glove box, seat, and carpets.
  6. Final Extraction: After all surfaces have been agitated and cleaned, perform the final hot water extraction on all carpets and fabric seats as needed.
Phase 3: The Finish
  1. Apply Protection: Apply dressings or premium ceramic coatings to all surfaces as required by the work order. (See Advanced Finishing Procedures).
  2. Clean Glass: Perform a professional, streak-free cleaning on all interior glass. (See Advanced Finishing Procedures).
  3. The TDS Signature Finish: Complete the final branding and presentation touches. (See Advanced Finishing Procedures).

Procedure: The Basic Interior Detail

A streamlined process for well-maintained vehicles. This service is not intended for heavy soil, stains, or excessive pet hair. It is a maintenance service to refresh a clean interior, not restore a dirty one. The goal is a clean, dust-free, and protected finish on all hard surfaces.

  1. Remove large items and debris.
  2. Blow out the entire vehicle with an air gun, working from back to front.
  3. Thoroughly vacuum all carpets, seats, and mats.
  4. Using CARPRO InnerQD, wipe down and protect all hard surfaces (dash, doors, console).
  5. Clean all interior glass with CARPRO Clarify.
  6. Perform the TDS Signature Finish steps (mat, freshener, card).

Procedure: Ozone Generator for Severe Odor Removal

CRITICAL SAFETY PROTOCOL

Ozone is highly effective but TOXIC to humans and pets. Inhaling it can cause severe respiratory damage. This procedure must be followed exactly.

  1. Ensure the interior is 100% clean, dry, and free of any loose organic material.
  2. Place the ozone generator inside the vehicle and seal any openings.
  3. Set the timer (typically 30-60 minutes) and start the machine.
  4. Place a prominent warning sign on the driver's window: "OZONE TREATMENT IN PROGRESS - DO NOT ENTER".
  5. After treatment, allow the ozone to dissipate for at least 1-2 hours before opening doors.
  6. Ventilate the vehicle with all doors open for a **minimum of 30 minutes** before anyone enters.

    1. Surface must be perfectly clean and free of oils. After cleaning with Inside, perform a final wipe with CARPRO Eraser to ensure a pure surface for bonding.
    2. Apply a few drops of Leather 2.0 to the included suede applicator.
    3. Wipe a thin, even layer onto a small section of leather (e.g., one seat bolster).
    4. Immediately, but gently, wipe away all excess residue with a clean microfiber towel. The goal is to leave no visible product behind, just protection.

Procedure: Professional Glass Cleaning

Method 1: Squeegee (Heavily Soiled)
  1. Spray CARPRO Clarify liberally onto the glass.
  2. Use a dedicated glass scrub pad to agitate and break loose any film or grime.
  3. Using a small squeegee, pull from top to bottom, wiping the blade after each pass.
  4. Use a clean, dry glass towel to wipe any remaining streaks from the edges.
Method 2: Two-Towel (Maintenance)
  1. Take a dedicated, low-pile glass cleaning towel. Lightly mist it with CARPRO Clarify.
  2. Wipe the interior of the glass thoroughly to remove fingerprints and light dust.
  3. Immediately, with a second, clean and dry glass cleaning towel (a waffle weave works best), buff the glass to a perfectly clear, streak-free finish.

Common Pitfalls & Delicate Surface Care

  • Alcantara: NEVER use a harsh cleaner or a stiff brush. Use a dedicated Alcantara cleaner, spray it onto a soft brush or towel (not the surface), gently wipe, and follow with a clean, damp microfiber towel.
  • Perforated Leather: To avoid clogging the holes, never spray cleaner directly onto the seat. Always spray onto your brush or towel first.
  • Piano Black Trim & Screens: These scratch very easily. Use a high-quality interior quick detailer like CARPRO InnerQD with a plush, clean microfiber towel only. Never use a paper towel or a dirty rag.

Final Inspection & Customer Handoff

The Interior Final Inspection Checklist

  • Surface Check: Are all plastic/vinyl surfaces free of streaky dressing?
  • Crevice Check: Shine a light deep into seat tracks, vents, and seams to ensure no dust or debris remains.
  • Glass Check: View the interior glass from the outside to spot any remaining streaks or haze.
  • Sensory Check: Does the car smell clean and neutral? Are all floor mats and personal items back in their proper place?
