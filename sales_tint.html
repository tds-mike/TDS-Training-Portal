Selling Window Tint

1.1 What is Window Tint?

Window tint is a multi-layered polyester film applied to the interior of a vehicle's windows. Its primary purpose is to reduce the amount of visible light, heat, and UV radiation that passes through the glass.

Core Customer Benefits:

  • Heat & Glare Reduction: Keeps the cabin significantly cooler and improves driving safety.
  • UV Protection: Blocks over 99% of harmful UV rays, protecting occupants and preventing interior fading.
  • Privacy & Security: Conceals valuables and enhances personal privacy.
  • Aesthetic Enhancement: Gives any vehicle a sleek, premium appearance.

1.3 Sales Outline: Matching Customer Needs

Use a consultative approach to guide the customer to the best solution for them. Listen first, then recommend.

  1. Ask Discovery Questions: Start by understanding their motivation.
    • "What are your primary reasons for wanting window tint today?"
    • "Are you looking more for heat reduction, privacy, or the appearance?"
  2. Educate on Film Types: Briefly explain the performance difference between Carbon and Ceramic films, focusing on the benefits relevant to their answers.
  3. Discuss Aesthetics & Legality: Inform them of the legal tint limits in Connecticut for their specific vehicle type (Sedan vs. SUV).
  4. Make a Recommendation: Based on their priorities (e.g., maximum heat rejection vs. budget), recommend either Ceramic or Carbon film as the best solution.
  5. Provide a Custom Solution: Confirm the specific windows they want tinted and provide a clear, confident quote.

1.4 Connecticut Tint Law & Medical Exemptions

It is our responsibility to inform customers of these legal regulations to ensure compliance.

  • Windshield: Non-reflective tint allowed only above the manufacturer's AS-1 line.
  • Front Side Windows: Must allow more than 35% of light in (35% VLT).
  • Rear Windows (Sedans): Must also allow more than 35% of light in.
  • Rear Windows (SUVs/Vans/Trucks): Any darkness can be used.
  • Medical Exemptions: A valid medical exemption allows for tint as dark as 20% VLT.
