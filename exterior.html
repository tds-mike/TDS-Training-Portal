The Crucial Role of Decontamination

A vehicle's paint is constantly bombarded with contaminants. Attempting to polish a contaminated surface is like rubbing sandpaper on the paint, inflicting deeper swirls. A thorough chemical and mechanical decontamination is the non-negotiable first step to any true paint enhancement. It creates a truly clean slate, allowing polishes to work correctly and coatings to bond permanently.

Goal: To remove ALL above and below-surface bonded contaminants. Time: 45-90 minutes.

  1. Inspection & Strategy: Use a swirl finder light to assess the paint's condition and identify problem areas. Perform the "baggie test" for roughness.
  2. Wheels, Tires & Wells First: Clean these first to prevent slinging grime onto clean panels.
  3. Touchless Pre-Soak: Foam the vehicle with CARPRO Lift (1:10). Let dwell for 5-10 minutes (do not let dry). Rinse thoroughly.
  4. Two-Bucket Contact Wash: Using a clean mitt and CARPRO Reset, wash following the strict Wash Order (WO): Roof → Glass → Hood/Trunk → Upper Sides → Front/Rear Bumpers → Lower Sides.
  5. Chemical Decontamination: While wet, apply CARPRO IronX, let it turn purple, then rinse. Apply CARPRO TarX to a microfiber applicator to remove tar deposits.
  6. Mechanical Decontamination: Use a clay lubricant (Ech2o 1:40) and glide a clay towel over the surface with zero pressure until it feels glass-smooth.
  7. Final Rinse & Drying: Perform a thorough final rinse. Dry using an air blower and a plush drying towel.

  2. Clean wheels and tires using the standard procedure.
  3. Use a foam cannon with a pH-neutral soap like CARPRO Reset to provide lubrication.

Paint Correction: The Art of Restoring Clarity

MANDATORY: Initial Diagnosis Protocol

Before any machine touches the paint, a full diagnosis must be performed. This is a non-negotiable safety and strategy step.

1. Using Light for Initial Diagnosis

A swirl finder light is your most important diagnostic tool. Before quoting a job or starting work, inspect every panel to understand the true condition of the paint. This allows you to identify the severity of defects (light swirls vs. deep scratches) and create an effective correction plan.

2. The Paint Depth Gauge (PDG) Protocol
  • Procedure: Take 3-5 readings on each major panel to get an average thickness.
  • Interpreting Readings:
    • 130µm+: Healthy factory paint.
    • 90-130µm: Thinner paint. Proceed with caution.
    • Below 90µm: Dangerously thin. Do NOT compound.
    • Over 160µm / Inconsistent: Indicates a repaint.

Workflow & Tool Prep

Efficiency and consistency come from preparation. Set up your workspace before you begin correcting.

  • Set Up Your Cart: Your detailing cart should be equipped with your polishers, a stack of clean pads for each step, various polishes/compounds, and a collection of clean microfiber towels.
  • Prepare Pad Washer: Ensure the Lake Country pad washer is filled with clean water and a pad cleaning solution. This should be ready to use throughout the process.
  • Organize Your Tools: Have your masking tape, inspection lights, and any other necessary tools readily available on your cart to minimize downtime.

Procedure: Level 1 - The Paint Enhancement (AIO)

Goal: To significantly improve gloss and clarity while adding a layer of protection in one step. This is not for heavy defect removal.

  • Product/Pad Combo: CARPRO Essence on a White Gloss Pad.
  • Technique: Follow the Core DA Polisher Technique with a faster arm speed (4-5 inches per second).
Procedure: Level 2 - The One-Step Correction

Goal: To remove light to moderate swirls (~70%) in a single polishing stage.

  • Product/Pad Combo: CARPRO Fixer or Reflect on an Orange Foam Pad.
  • Technique: Follow the Core DA Polisher Technique with a slow arm speed (1-2 inches per second) to allow the abrasives to fully break down.
Procedure: Level 3 - The Two-Step Correction

Goal: To remove medium to heavy defects (~85%) by using separate compounding and polishing stages.

  • Step 1 (Compounding): Use CARPRO UltraCut on a Microfiber/Wool Pad.
  • Step 2 (Polishing): Use CARPRO Reflect on an Orange/White Foam Pad to refine the finish.
Procedure: Level 4 - Wetsanding Correction

Goal: The highest level of correction for deep scratches and severe texture (~99% defect removal). This is an advanced technique.

  • Sanding Stage: Use a sequence of grits (e.g., 1500 -> 3000) to level the defect.
  • Correction Stage: A full **Level 3 - Two-Step Correction** must be performed to remove sanding marks and restore gloss.

The Core Technique: Using a DA Polisher

Watch: DA Polisher Technique

This video demonstrates the correct pressure, arm speed, and cross-hatch pattern for effective and safe paint correction.

[Video Tutorial Placeholder]

  1. Section the Panel: Mentally divide the panel into 2'x2' sections.
  2. Prime the Pad: For a fresh pad, apply 4-6 pea-sized drops of product. For subsequent sections, add 2-3 drops.
  3. Spread the Product: On speed 1-2, quickly spread the product over the section without pressure.
  4. The Correction Pass: Increase speed to 3-4. Apply about 5-10 lbs of pressure. Move the polisher slowly in a cross-hatch pattern (3 passes up/down, 3 passes left/right).
  5. Clean Pad On-The-Fly: After each section, use the Lake Country pad washer to clean your pad. A clean pad cuts more effectively and produces a better finish.
  6. Wipe & Inspect: Turn off the machine, wipe residue with a plush microfiber, and inspect with a swirl-finder light before moving to the next section.

Post-Correction Cleanup

The job isn't finished until the vehicle is perfectly clean and ready for the protection step.

  • Remove Tape: Carefully peel away all masking tape.
  • Purge Crevices: Use compressed air or a detail brush to remove any dried compound dust from panel gaps, emblem edges, and window seals.
  • Final Wipe-Down: Meticulously wipe down the entire vehicle with CARPRO Eraser to remove all polishing oils. This is a critical step to ensure a pure surface for waxes, sealants, or coatings to bond to. Use multiple clean towels.

Advanced Techniques & Professional Habits

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Burning Paint on Edges: Body lines and edges build heat much faster. Use very light pressure and fewer passes.
  • Ignoring Repaints: If the PDG indicates a repaint, you MUST perform a small test spot. Record any issues in the Urable app inspection, and report to the shop manager before proceeding.

Pad Maintenance

Proper pad care extends their life and ensures consistent results.

  • Clean After Use: After a job is complete, thoroughly clean all used pads in the pad washer before storing them.
  • Store Properly: Store pads in a clean, dry location away from dust and debris to ensure they are ready for the next job.

Procedure: Ceramic Coating Application

Watch: Ceramic Coating Application

Learn how to identify the "flash," the correct technique for leveling, and how to spot and fix high spots before they cure.

[Video Tutorial Placeholder]

  1. Final Prep is Paramount: Meticulously wipe down the entire vehicle with CARPRO Eraser. This step is critical for a proper bond.
  2. Application: Work in 2'x2' sections. Apply coating to a suede applicator and spread in a criss-cross pattern.
  3. Observe the "Flash": After 2-5 minutes, you will see a "rainbow" effect. This is the signal to begin removal.
  4. Leveling & Removal: With a short-nap microfiber, perform an initial wipe to "level" the coating. Immediately follow with a second, plush microfiber to remove all residue.
  5. Inspect for High Spots: Use an inspection light to check for dark smudges (high spots). If caught within minutes, they can be removed by re-applying coating and immediately wiping off. If cured, they must be polished off.

Final Inspection

Using Light for a Flawless Inspection

A perfect finish can only be confirmed with proper lighting. Use multiple light sources to catch different types of defects.

  • Direct Light (Swirl Finder): The focused, intense beam is essential for identifying swirls, scratches, and rotary holograms.
  • Diffused Light (Large Ceiling Panels): This soft, broad light is best for spotting ceramic coating high spots and ensuring a uniform, streak-free finish.
  • Angled Light: Holding your light at a low angle to the panel can help reveal any remaining light haze or fine micro-marring from the final polishing step.
