Coating Sales Phone Call Playbook

This is your step-by-step guide for conducting a successful sales call for ceramic coatings. Follow this framework to diagnose customer needs, build immense value, and confidently book an appointment.

1. The NEPQ Phone Call Framework

Step 1: Connecting Questions

Goal: Build initial rapport and understand their motivation for calling.

Example Questions:

  • "Thanks for calling The Detail Shop! To get started, what was it about ceramic coatings that interested you enough to call us today?"
  • "I see you submitted a request on our website. What are you hoping to achieve for your car?"
  • "Excellent, coatings are the best thing you can do for a new car. What kind of vehicle are we talking about today?"
  • "Before we dive into pricing, can you tell me a bit about what prompted you to look into professional protection?"
  • "I'm happy to help with that. What's the main goal you have for your car's paint?"

Step 2: Situation Questions

Goal: Understand their current reality and relationship with their car.

Example Questions:

  • "Tell me a bit about your car. Is it brand new or have you had it for a while?"
  • "How are you currently maintaining the paint? Do you wash it yourself or take it somewhere?"
  • "Is this a daily driver you take on the highway, or more of a weekend car?"
  • "Where is the vehicle usually parked? In a garage or outside?"
  • "What did you do to protect the paint on your last vehicle?"

Step 3: Problem Awareness Questions

Goal: Help the customer articulate their specific frustrations (their "problem"). This is the most important step.

Example Questions:

  • "What's the most frustrating part about trying to keep your car's paint looking the way you want?"
  • "Have you noticed any issues like water spots, swirl marks, or the shine fading quickly after a wash?"
  • "How does it make you feel when you spend all that time washing it, just for it to look dirty again the next day?"
  • "On your last dark-colored car, what was the biggest challenge in keeping it looking good?"
  • "When you look at the paint in the direct sun, what are the little imperfections you see that bother you the most?"

Step 4: Solution Awareness Questions

Goal: Get the client to start thinking about a future where their problem is solved.

Example Questions:

  • "What have you looked into so far to solve that issue with the paint getting dull?"
  • "Ideally, what would the perfect solution for keeping your car clean and protected look like for you?"
  • "What are you hoping the ceramic coating will do for you and your car?"
  • "Have you seen a coated car in person? What was it that stood out to you?"
  • "If you could wave a magic wand, what's the one thing you'd change about maintaining your car?"

Step 5: Consequence Questions

Goal: Help the client understand the negative consequences (logical and emotional) of not taking action.

Example Questions:

  • "What happens if you don't do anything to protect the paint? How do you think it will look a year from now?"
  • "If you continue spending your weekends washing and waxing the car, what other things are you giving up?"
  • "What's the long-term impact on your car's resale value if the paint becomes swirled and faded?"
  • "How will you feel a year from now if the car looks just as dull as your last one did?"
  • "If the water spots and bug stains continue, what permanent damage could that do to the clear coat?"

Step 6: Qualifying Questions

Goal: Determine how important it is for the client to solve this problem *now*.

Example Questions:

  • "On a scale of 1-10, how important is it for you to solve this problem and keep your car protected?"
  • "Why is getting this handled now a priority for you, instead of waiting a few more months?"
  • "What's your timeline for getting this protection applied?"
  • "Is this something you've budgeted for as part of the new vehicle purchase?"
  • "How ready are you to finally find a permanent solution to this problem?"

Step 7: Transition Questions

Goal: Bridge the gap from diagnosis to your recommendation, summarizing their problem and teeing up your solution.

Example Transition Statements:

  • "Okay, so based on what you've told me about the constant frustration of trying to keep your car clean and your desire to protect your new investment, it sounds like our 5-Year Ceramic Coating could be the perfect permanent solution for you. Does that feel like it might be the right answer?"
  • "It seems like your main goal is to get that incredible show-car gloss and protect it from the swirls that drive you crazy. With that in mind, our DQUARTZ coating is likely the best fit. Would you agree?"
  • "Given that your priority is long-term protection without the hassle of waxing, I'm confident our CQUARTZ Professional package is the solution you're looking for. Does that sound right to you?"

Step 8: Committing Questions

Goal: Secure a commitment for the next logical step: scheduling an in-person consultation or the actual service.

Key Questions:

  • "Do you feel like this could be the answer for you?"
  • "The next step would be to get you on the schedule for the installation. Would that be appropriate?"
  • "I have an opening next Tuesday at 9 AM. Would you like to book that spot?"
  • "What would you like the next step to be?"
  • "Are you ready to move forward with getting this scheduled?"

2. Putting It All Together: The Ideal Phone Call Flow

The Goal of the First Call: Get the Appointment

You cannot effectively sell a high-value, visual service over the phone. The primary objective of the initial call is to use NEPQ to build enough value and trust to get the customer to come to the shop for an in-person consultation, where you can properly diagnose their vehicle and demonstrate our solutions.

View Full Conversation Example

Salesperson: "Thanks for calling The Detail Shop! This is [Your Name]. How can I help?"

Client: "Hi, I just got a new black SUV and I'm interested in a ceramic coating."

[Connecting] Salesperson: "Excellent, congratulations on the new vehicle! You're calling about the best thing you can possibly do to protect it. To make sure I give you the right info, what was it about coatings that you were most interested in?"

Client: "I just want to keep it looking new and shiny. I've heard they make it easier to wash."

[Situation] Salesperson: "They absolutely do. And since it's brand new, how are you planning on maintaining it right now?"

Client: "Probably just washing it on the weekends."

[Problem Awareness] Salesperson: "That makes sense. What's been your biggest frustration with keeping past cars, especially black ones, looking good?"

Client: "The swirl marks! And it looks dirty five minutes after I wash it. It drives me crazy."

Playbook Cue: If the client expresses strong emotion ("drives me crazy"), they are a great candidate for an upsell. See the Upsell Playbook (Section 5) for the "Enthusiast" script.

[Consequence] Salesperson: "I hear that. And what's the consequence of that frustration? Does it make you feel like all that hard work washing it was a waste of time?"

Client: "Yes, exactly! It feels like a losing battle."

[Qualifying] Salesperson: "So it sounds like finding a permanent solution to that 'losing battle' is a big priority for you then?"

Client: "It is. I want to enjoy this car, not be a slave to it."

Playbook Cue: If the client objects to price or stalls at this point, refer to the Objection Handling Playbook (Section 4).

[Transition] Salesperson: "Well, based on that, a ceramic coating is the perfect answer. It will give you that insane gloss and make it so much easier to clean, which solves the exact problems you're talking about. The next step is to have you bring the vehicle by for a 10-minute in-person consultation so we can inspect the paint and show you our coating options."

[Committing] Salesperson: "I have some time this afternoon or tomorrow morning. Which works better for you?"

3. Adapting the Script: Persona-Based Conversation Flows

While the ideal flow provides a great foundation, a skilled salesperson adapts their language to the customer's specific motivations. Once you identify their persona, tailor your questions to focus on what matters most to them.

Script for "The New Car Owner" (Focus: Preservation)

Goal: Validate their purchase and frame the coating as an essential step in preserving their new investment.

Salesperson: "Congratulations on the new car! You're calling at the perfect time. What's the number one thing you want to protect on your new investment?"

Client: "I just want to keep that new car shine. My last car looked so faded after a few years."

[Problem Awareness] Salesperson: "That's a very common frustration. That fading is caused by UV damage, and it's exactly what a ceramic coating is designed to prevent. It acts like a permanent, high-gloss layer of sunscreen for your paint."

[Transition] Salesperson: "It sounds like locking in that 'better than new' finish for the long haul is your main goal. Does our 5-year coating feel like the right level of permanent protection for your new investment?"

Script for "The Busy Professional" (Focus: Convenience)

Goal: Sell them back their free time by focusing on the low-maintenance benefits.

Salesperson: "Thanks for calling! What's the biggest headache for you when it comes to keeping your car looking good?"

Client: "Honestly, I just have no time to wash it. It's always dirty and it's frustrating."

[Consequence Question] Salesperson: "I hear that a lot. And what's the consequence of that lack of time? Do you end up driving a car you're not proud of, or do you have to sacrifice your weekend to get it clean?"

Client: "Both! I'd rather be doing anything else on a Saturday than washing my car."

[Solution Awareness] Salesperson: "That's the exact problem a coating solves. Because it's so slick, your car will stay cleaner for much longer, and when you do wash it, it's a quick, effortless process. You're essentially buying back your weekends. How valuable would that be for you?"

Script for "The Enthusiast" (Focus: Aesthetics & Performance)

Goal: Speak their language. Focus on the visual impact and position the top-tier coatings as the only real choice for a true car lover.

Salesperson: "Thanks for calling! Are you looking to maximize the gloss and protection on your [Car Model]?"

Client: "Yes, I want that deep, wet look, and I'm tired of seeing swirl marks."

[Problem Awareness] Salesperson: "You've called the right place. You know as well as I do that swirl marks are the enemy of a perfect finish. What's the most frustrating part of that for you as an owner?"

Client: "No matter how careful I am washing it, they seem to come back. It's impossible to keep it perfect."

Playbook Cue: This is a prime candidate for DQUARTZ. Jump to the Upsell Playbook (Section 5) and use the DQUARTZ script.

[Transition] Salesperson: "That's where our professional coatings come in. We can polish the paint to a flawless state and then apply a coating that's significantly harder than your clear coat, giving you the best possible defense against those swirls. To achieve the level of perfection you're looking for, our CQUARTZ Professional or even our DQUARTZ coating is going to be the answer."

4. Objection Handling Playbook

An objection is not a "no"—it's a request for more information. Use the "Clarify, Discuss, Diffuse" method to turn these moments into opportunities to build more value.

Objection: "It costs more than I expected."

Client: "Okay, that's a bit more than I was expecting."

[Clarify] Salesperson: "I understand. And just so I'm clear, is the concern more about the total investment today, or are you wondering about the value you get for that price?"

[Discuss] Salesperson: "That's completely fair. The price reflects not just the coating itself, but the 8-10 hours of meticulous paint correction we perform beforehand to make the paint flawless. You're investing in a permanent solution, not a temporary shine."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "When you consider that you'll never have to wax the car again and it will cut your cleaning time in half for years to come, does the investment feel more justified?"

Objection: "I need to talk to my spouse."

Client: "This all sounds great, but I need to check with my wife before I book anything."

[Clarify] Salesperson: "That's a great idea. So you have all the info for that conversation, what do you think her number one question will be?"

[Discuss] Salesperson: "It's often the cost. It might be helpful to frame it as a long-term investment that protects the value of the car and saves a lot of time on maintenance, which means more family time on weekends."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "How about we schedule a tentative appointment? That reserves your spot, and you can just call me to confirm or cancel after you've talked. No obligation at all. How does that sound?"

Objection: "My car is brand new, does it really need paint correction?"

Client: "I want the coating, but do I really need to pay for paint correction on a brand new car?"

[Clarify] Salesperson: "That's the most logical question to ask, and the answer surprises most people. Are you available to swing by the shop for 10 minutes? I can show you exactly why it's so important."

[Discuss & Educate with Proof] Salesperson: (In person) "If you look here under my light, you can see this fine webbing of scratches. This happens at the dealership during their quick wash process. A coating is a semi-permanent layer, so it will lock in these scratches if we don't remove them first."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "We have to create a truly perfect, flawless surface to bond the coating to. You're investing in a perfect finish, so it makes sense to ensure the foundation is perfect first, right?"

Objection: "I'll think about it."

Client: "Okay, thank you. I need some time to think about it."

[Clarify] Salesperson: "Of course, it's a big decision. Just so I know what information would be most helpful for you while you're thinking, what's the biggest question still on your mind?"

Client: "Just the overall cost."

[Discuss] Salesperson: "I understand. One thing many of our clients consider is our financing option, which can break the investment down into small, manageable monthly payments. It often makes it much easier to get the level of protection you really want."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "Would exploring that financing option be helpful for you?"

5. Upsell Playbook: The Value Ladder

Focus on selling the tangible benefits of each tier, not just the years of durability. Guide the customer up the ladder based on the problems they've told you they want to solve.

The Coating Value Ladder: Good, Better, Best, Ultimate

  • CQUARTZ Lite (Good): Solves "My car is never shiny." Provides great gloss and makes the car easy to clean.
  • CQUARTZ SiC (Better): Solves "My paint feels rough and water spots are an issue." Adds superior chemical resistance, slickness, and gloss.
  • CQUARTZ Professional (Best): Solves "I want the deepest, richest look possible." Adds a harder layer for increased gloss, richness, and swirl resistance.
  • DQUARTZ Professional (Ultimate): Solves "I hate swirl marks from washing." Adds the maximum possible scratch resistance for the true perfectionist.
Upsell Path #1: Inquiry to CQUARTZ SiC (The Core Offer)

Goal: Establish CQUARTZ SiC as the primary recommendation for most customers, positioning Lite as a budget alternative.

Salesperson: "Based on your goal of long-term protection and making the car easy to clean, our core recommendation is CQUARTZ SiC. This is our most popular coating and for good reason. It provides a fantastic balance of insane gloss, slickness, and strong protection against things like water spots and bird droppings. This is the true 'set-it-and-forget-it' solution for most drivers."

Upsell Path #2: CQUARTZ SiC to CQUARTZ Professional

Goal: Guide the customer who wants more than the standard to the next level by focusing on aesthetics and feel.

Salesperson: "The CQUARTZ SiC is an excellent workhorse coating. Now, for clients who are looking for that extra level of visual impact, we have CQUARTZ Professional. The main difference you'll see and feel is an even deeper, richer gloss that really makes the paint color pop. It also adds another level of hardness for better swirl resistance. For an enthusiast, it's a very noticeable step up in the finish."

[Committing Question] Salesperson: "Is achieving that absolute maximum level of gloss and richness a priority for you?"

Upsell Path #3: CQUARTZ Professional to DQUARTZ Professional

Goal: Guide an enthusiast client to the absolute best protection by focusing on their specific pain point: swirl marks.

Salesperson: "Excellent, CQUARTZ Professional is a phenomenal choice. Now, since you're clearly someone who hates swirl marks, I have to show you our ultimate option: DQUARTZ. Think of it as taking that armor and lacing it with diamonds. Its primary benefit is the absolute best resistance to the wash-induced swirls that drive enthusiasts crazy. To ensure your finish stays as flawless as possible for as long as possible, does it make sense to go with the DQUARTZ upgrade?"

Upsell Path #4: Adding Total Surface Protection

Goal: Expand the sale by protecting other high-wear surfaces after the paint coating has been sold.

Salesperson (Wheels): "Now that the paint is protected, let's talk about the dirtiest part of your car: the wheels. You know how brake dust is like a magnet and so hard to clean? Our wheel coating creates a slick barrier that brake dust can't stick to, making them rinse clean."

Salesperson (Glass): "For the windshield, our glass coating is a huge safety feature. In the rain, water flies right off the glass, so you barely need to use your wipers, which dramatically improves visibility."

Salesperson (Interior): "And for the inside, our interior coatings protect against spills and UV fading on your leather and fabrics. It's like Scotchgard on steroids."

[Committing Question] Salesperson: "Does it make sense to add these for a complete, total-vehicle protection package?"
