PPF Sales Phone Call Playbook

This playbook is your guide to selling peace of mind. Use this NEPQ-based framework to uncover a customer's anxiety about paint damage and position PPF as the ultimate solution.

1. The NEPQ Phone Call Framework for PPF

Step 1 & 2: Connecting & Situation

Goal: Understand why they're considering PPF and their driving habits.

Example Questions:

  • "Thanks for calling! PPF is the best way to protect your investment. What got you thinking about it for your car?"
  • "What kind of driving do you do most often? Are you frequently on the highway?"
  • "Is this a brand new vehicle, or are you looking to protect a car you've had for a while?"
  • "Have you had PPF before? What was your experience?"
  • "What's the main thing you're looking to protect your car from?"

Step 3: Problem Awareness

Goal: Help them voice their specific fears about paint damage.

Example Questions:

  • "What's your biggest concern when you're driving on the highway behind a truck?"
  • "How would you feel if you walked out to your brand new car and saw the first rock chip on the hood?"
  • "On your last car, what did the front end look like after a few years of daily driving?"
  • "Besides rock chips, what other kinds of damage are you worried about, like scratches in parking lots?"
  • "How much does that first inevitable scratch or chip take away from the excitement of owning a new car?"

Step 4: Solution Awareness

Goal: Get them thinking about a future where their problem is solved.

Example Questions:

  • "What have you looked into so far to solve that issue with rock chips?"
  • "Ideally, what would the perfect solution for protecting your car's paint look like for you?"
  • "What are you hoping the paint protection film will do for you and your car?"

Step 5: Consequence Questions

Goal: Solidify the negative outcome of not protecting the vehicle.

Example Questions:

  • "What's the long-term impact on your car's resale value if the front end gets peppered with rock chips?"
  • "If you don't protect the paint now, what's the cost and hassle of having to repaint a panel down the road?"
  • "How does that constant worry about getting a chip affect how you enjoy driving the car?"

Step 6: Qualifying Questions

Goal: Determine how important it is for the client to solve this problem *now*.

Example Questions:

  • "How important is it for you to protect your new car from chips and scratches before they happen?"
  • "Why is getting this handled now a priority for you, while the paint is still in perfect condition?"
  • "Are you ready to find a permanent solution to the anxiety of driving on the highway?"
  • "If we can find a solution that fits your needs, how soon would you want to get it installed?"

Step 7: Transition Questions

Goal: Bridge the gap from diagnosis to your recommendation.

Example Transition Statements:

  • "Okay, based on your concern about rock chips and wanting to preserve that new car feeling, it sounds like our Full Front PPF package is the exact solution you need for peace of mind. Does that feel like the right answer for you?"
  • "It seems like your main goal is to protect your investment and avoid the cost and hassle of a repaint down the road. With that in mind, I'm confident our film is the solution you're looking for. Would you agree?"

Step 8: Committing Questions

Goal: Secure a commitment for the next logical step: booking an appointment.

Key Questions:

  • "Do you feel like this could be the answer for you?"
  • "The next step is to get you scheduled for the installation. I have an opening next week, would you like to reserve that spot?"
  • "Are you ready to move forward and get your car protected?"

2. Putting It All Together: The Ideal Phone Call Flow

The Goal of the First Call: Get the Appointment

The PPF demo kit (wire brush & self-healing demo) is your closing tool. The phone call's purpose is to tap into their anxiety about damage and get them to the shop to see the solution in person.

View Full Conversation Example

Salesperson: "Thanks for your interest in our paint protection film. What kind of car are you looking to protect?"

Client: "I just got a new Porsche 911."

[Connecting] Salesperson: "That's a beautiful car, congratulations! You're making a very smart call; protecting that factory paint from day one is the most important thing you can do."

Playbook Cue: This is a classic "Enthusiast" persona. Jump to the "Enthusiast" script in Section 3.

[Problem Awareness] Salesperson: "What's the number one thing you're worried about when it comes to damaging that new paint?"

Client: "Rock chips. I absolutely hate them. My last car was covered in them."

[Consequence] Salesperson: "I understand completely. It's not a matter of *if*, but *when* that first chip happens on our roads. How would it feel to walk out to that beautiful new 911 and see a rock chip right in the middle of the hood?"

Client: "I'd be devastated."

Playbook Cue: If the client objects to price or stalls at this point, refer to the Objection Handling Playbook (Section 4).

[Transition] Salesperson: "That's the exact feeling our PPF is designed to prevent. It's an invisible, self-healing armor for your paint. The best way to understand its strength is to see our demo. We have a panel we can try to scratch with a wire brush right in front of you. Are you available to stop by for 5-10 minutes today or tomorrow?"

3. Adapting the Script: Persona-Based Conversation Flows

Script for "The Daily Commuter"

Salesperson: "You mentioned you're on the highway every day for work. What kind of debris do you typically see getting kicked up on your commute?"

Client: "Everything. Sand, rocks, you name it. My last car's bumper was a mess."

[Transition] Salesperson: "That's exactly why our Full Front package is so popular with commuters. It armors the entire front of your car against that daily assault. The best way to understand how it works is to see it. Are you free to stop by for a quick 5-minute demo?"

Script for "The Enthusiast"

Salesperson: "For a performance car like yours, I'm sure you want to keep that factory paint absolutely pristine. Are you planning any track days or spirited drives with it?"

Client: "Definitely. That's why I'm calling. I'm worried about rubber from the track and rock chips."

[Transition] Salesperson: "That's a smart move. Our Track Pack is designed for exactly that. It covers the full front end and adds protection to the rocker panels to stop debris kicked up by your own tires. It's the go-to package for ensuring you can enjoy the drive without worrying about the paint."

Script for "The Family Hauler"

Salesperson: "With the kids and all the gear, I bet the car takes a beating in more places than just the front bumper, right?"

Client: "You have no idea! The area behind the door handles is all scratched up, and the top of the rear bumper is a mess from dragging things out."

[Solution] Salesperson: "That's a very common issue. Along with protecting the front end, we have custom-fit pieces specifically for those high-wear areas like door cups, door edges, and a luggage strip for the rear bumper. They're small additions that solve a huge headache. We can go over those when you come in for your consultation."

4. PPF Objection Handling Playbook

Objection: "Will it turn yellow?"

[Clarify & Educate] Salesperson: "That's a very common concern based on older films from 10-15 years ago. The modern films we use are completely different. They have powerful UV inhibitors built into the top coat, which is why they come with a 10-year manufacturer's warranty specifically against yellowing or cracking."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "Does knowing your investment is backed by a decade-long warranty give you the peace of mind that this is a permanent, high-quality solution?"

Objection: "It seems expensive for a piece of plastic."

[Clarify] Salesperson: "I understand the investment. Is your concern the upfront cost, or are you wondering what makes it so much more than a simple vinyl wrap?"

[Discuss with Analogy] Salesperson: "It's helpful to think of it less as plastic and more as an insurance policy for your factory paint. Repainting a single bumper to match the factory finish after it's been damaged by rocks can cost over $1,000. Our film is a one-time investment to ensure you never have that cost or the headache of dealing with a body shop."

Objection: "Can't I just touch up rock chips?"

[Clarify] Salesperson: "You definitely can, and touch-up paint is great for a single, isolated chip. But when you get dozens of tiny chips peppered across the front end, are you realistically going to touch up every single one?"

[Discuss] Salesperson: "Even when you do, it never looks quite right; you can always see the little blobs of paint. PPF is the only solution that prevents the damage from happening in the first place, preserving that perfect, smooth factory finish."

Objection: "I'm just shopping around."

[Clarify] Salesperson: "That's a smart move. When you're comparing quotes, it's important to know what you're looking for. Are other shops telling you about the specific brand of film they use, or if they wrap the edges for a seamless look?"

[Discuss & Educate] Salesperson: "The details make all the difference. We use premium films that come with a 10-year warranty against yellowing. More importantly, our expert installers take the time to wrap the film around the edges of panels, so the final result is virtually invisible. A cheaper price often means exposed edges that can collect dirt and peel."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "As you compare, would you agree that the quality of the installation and the invisibility of the final product are just as important as the initial price?"

5. PPF Upsell Playbook

The PPF Value Ladder: Good, Better, Best

  • Partial Front (Good): Solves the immediate problem of high-impact zones.
  • Full Front (Better): Solves the aesthetic problem of visible lines for a seamless look.
  • Track Pack / Full Vehicle (Best): Solves the problem of total peace of mind for the true enthusiast.
Upsell Path #1: Partial Front to Full Front End

Salesperson: "The Partial Front is a great start that protects the highest impact areas. However, for a car like yours, most of our clients opt for the Full Front package. The main reason is aesthetics—you will have a visible line where the film ends across your hood and fenders with the partial kit. For a completely seamless, invisible look, the Full Front package is the way to go. It covers the entire hood and fenders, so there are no lines, just perfect, uninterrupted protection."

Upsell Path #2: Adding High-Impact Add-Ons

Salesperson: "Now that we have the front end protected from rocks, let's think about everyday damage. The areas behind your door handles, the door edges, and the top of your rear bumper are the most commonly scratched parts of any car from keys, rings, bags, and groceries. We can add small, invisible pieces of film to those areas to prevent that kind of damage. It's a small addition that provides a huge amount of practical protection."

Upsell Path #3: Adding a Ceramic Coating

Salesperson: "Excellent, the PPF will give you unbeatable protection against rock chips. Now, while we have the car here and perfectly prepped, it's the ideal time to add a ceramic coating over the entire vehicle, including the film. This will give you that insane gloss and make the entire car, both the filmed and un-filmed sections, incredibly easy to clean. It's the ultimate combination of armor and shine."
