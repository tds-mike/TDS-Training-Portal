Tint Sales Phone Call Playbook

This is your step-by-step guide for conducting a successful sales call for window tint. Follow this framework to diagnose customer needs, build value around comfort and protection, and book an appointment.

1. The NEPQ Phone Call Framework for Tint

Step 1 & 2: Connecting & Situation

Goal: Understand their motivation and current situation.

Example Questions:

  • "To make sure I recommend the right film, are you looking for tint more for privacy, for heat rejection, or for the sleek appearance?"
  • "Tell me about your car. How often do you have passengers, especially kids, in the back seats?"
  • "What kind of driving do you do most often? Long highway commutes or mostly city driving?"
  • "Have you had tint on your previous cars? What did you like or dislike about it?"
  • "What's the main reason you decided to call about tint today?"
  • "How does the sun affect your driving experience, especially during peak hours?"

Step 3: Problem Awareness

Goal: Help them articulate the frustrations of driving without tint.

Example Questions:

  • "How uncomfortable does your car get after sitting in the sun for a few hours?"
  • "What's the most frustrating part about the sun glare during your morning or evening commute?"
  • "Have you ever felt like you were in a 'fishbowl' at a stoplight?"
  • "How much does the heat affect your kids or other passengers in the back seat?"
  • "Do you find your AC struggles to keep the car cool on really hot days?"
  • "Are you concerned about protecting your car's interior from UV damage?"
  • "How important is it for you to improve your car's comfort during the hot summer months?"

Step 4: Solution Awareness

Goal: Get them thinking about a future where their problem is solved.

Example Questions:

  • "What have you looked into so far to solve that issue with the heat?"
  • "Ideally, what would the perfect solution for making your car more comfortable look like for you?"
  • "What are you hoping the window tint will do for you and your family?"

Step 5: Consequence Questions

Goal: Solidify the negative outcome of not getting tint.

Example Questions:

  • "If you go another summer without tint, how will that affect your comfort on road trips?"
  • "What's the long-term impact of UV rays on your car's interior if you don't do anything to block them?"
  • "How does that constant glare affect your energy levels after a long drive?"

Step 6: Qualifying Questions

Goal: Determine how important it is for the client to solve this problem *now*.

Example Questions:

  • "How important is it for you to get this heat issue handled before the summer really kicks in?"
  • "What's your timeline for getting the car more comfortable for your daily commute?"
  • "Are you ready to find a permanent solution to that annoying glare?"
  • "If we can find a solution that fits your needs, how soon would you want to get it installed?"

Step 7: Transition Questions

Goal: Bridge the gap from diagnosis to your recommendation.

Example Transition Statements:

  • "Okay, so based on what you've told me about how uncomfortable the car gets and your desire to protect your kids from the sun, it sounds like our Supreme Ceramic film is the perfect solution. Does that feel like it might be the right answer for you?"
  • "It seems like your main goal is to finally solve that heat issue for good. With that in mind, I'm confident our ceramic film is the solution you're looking for. Would you agree?"

Step 8: Committing Questions

Goal: Secure a commitment for the next logical step: booking an appointment.

Key Questions:

  • "Do you feel like this could be the answer for you?"
  • "The next step is to get you on the schedule. I have an opening next Tuesday at 9 AM. Would you like to book that spot?"
  • "Are you ready to move forward with getting this scheduled?"

The Goal of the First Call: Get the Appointment

The heat lamp demo is your most powerful sales tool for tint. The goal of the call is to build enough value to get the customer to come feel the difference themselves.

4. Tint Objection Handling Playbook

Objection: "Why is ceramic so much more expensive?"

[Clarify] Salesperson: "That's a fair question. Are you wondering about the technology difference, or how that translates to real-world performance?"

[Discuss & Educate] Salesperson: "The price difference is purely in the material's ability to block heat. A standard film is great for looks, but the ceramic film has nano-ceramic particles that are specifically designed to block infrared heat. As you felt with our demo, that's the difference between a little relief and a huge drop in temperature."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "Since you said comfort was your top priority, does it make sense to invest in the film that actually solves that heat problem for you?"

Objection: "What about the dot matrix on my rear window?"

[Clarify & Educate] Salesperson: "I'm glad you brought that up. You're talking about the raised black dots around the edge of the glass, right? Those are baked on by the manufacturer. Because they aren't flat, the film can't make perfect contact, which can create a slightly silvery or wavy look in that area. This is completely normal and happens with every tint installation on this type of glass. Our technicians are experts at minimizing it, but it's an unavoidable part of the process."

Objection: "I'm just shopping around."

[Clarify] Salesperson: "I absolutely encourage it, it's smart to know your options. So I can give you the most accurate comparison, are you mostly comparing prices, or are you also looking at the type of film and the installation process other shops use?"

[Discuss & Educate] Salesperson: "The reason I ask is that not all tint jobs are created equal. A lower price often means a lower quality dyed film that can turn purple in a year, or a hand-cut installation that can scratch your glass. We only use color-stable Carbon and Ceramic films, and we use a computer plotter for a perfect, blade-free cut every time. That precision is what you're investing in."

[Diffuse] Salesperson: "When you're making your final decision, is the peace of mind that comes with a lifetime warranty and a perfect, blade-free installation important to you?"

5. Tint Upsell Playbook

The Tint Value Ladder: Good, Better, Best, Ultimate

  • Economy (Dyed): Solves "I just want my windows dark for privacy."
  • Premium Carbon: Solves "I want a quality look that won't fade or turn purple."
  • Supreme Ceramic: Solves "The heat in my car is unbearable in the summer."
  • CARPRO UVX: Solves "I want the absolute best UV protection for my family and interior, plus maximum heat rejection."
Upsell Path #1: Carbon to Ceramic Film

Salesperson: "The carbon film is a great choice for that factory look and will never fade. Now, since you mentioned the summer heat is a big concern for you, I have to show you the difference our ceramic film makes. For the difference in price, you're getting over double the heat rejection. It's an upgrade you'll physically feel every sunny day."

Upsell Path #2: Adding the Windshield

Salesperson: "Excellent, so we're all set for the ceramic tint on the side and rear windows. The biggest source of heat in any car is this huge piece of glass right in front of us. We have a virtually clear ceramic film designed specifically for windshields that blocks an incredible amount of heat without affecting your visibility at all. To get the maximum possible heat rejection and truly transform the comfort of your cabin, does it make sense to add the windshield as well?"
