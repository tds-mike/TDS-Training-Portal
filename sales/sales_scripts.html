Sales Scripts & Dialogues Playbook

This is the master playbook for customer interactions. These scripts are your guide for structuring conversations, from the initial call to the in-person consultation, ensuring you follow the NEPQ framework to uncover needs, build value, and close sales effectively.

Section 1: Initial Phone Inquiry Scripts

The goal of the first call is not just to give a price, but to start a diagnosis. Use these scripts to guide the conversation.

Standard Call Introduction

"Thanks for calling The Detail Shop, where we specialize in PPF, window tint, and ceramic coatings. This is [Your Name], who do I have the pleasure of speaking with?"

Script: Ceramic Coating Inquiry

  • Connecting Question: "Great! I'm happy to discuss ceramic coatings. To start, what was it about ceramic coatings that interested you enough to call us today?"
  • Situation Question: "Can you tell me a bit about your vehicle and how you typically maintain it?"
  • Problem Awareness Question: "What are some of the challenges you've faced in keeping your car's paint looking the way you want?"

Script: PPF Inquiry

  • Connecting Question: "I can certainly help with that. First, what prompted you to consider paint protection film for your vehicle?"
  • Situation Question: "What type of driving do you typically do? For example, are you mostly on the highway or city streets?"
  • Problem Awareness Question: "What are your primary concerns about potential damage to your vehicle's paint? Rock chips, scratches...?"

Script: Window Tint Inquiry

  • Connecting Question: "Excellent, I can help with a quote for tint. To give you the best recommendation, are you looking for tint more for privacy, for heat rejection, or for the sleek appearance?"
  • Educate Briefly: "Okay, based on that, we have two great options. Our carbon film is excellent for privacy and style, but our ceramic film is the best for rejecting heat. Which of those is a bigger priority for you?"

Section 3: The In-Person Consultation Script

This framework guides the walk-around and consultation, transitioning from diagnosis to a confident close.

  • The Warm Welcome: "Welcome to The Detail Shop, thanks for coming in! What are you hoping to achieve with your vehicle today?"
  • The Discovery: Use your NEPQ questions to identify their "why." Example: "Is your primary goal to protect the paint, enhance the look, or make the car easier to clean?"
  • The Vehicle Assessment: Walk around the vehicle with the client. Point out existing issues (swirls, chips) and use this as a teaching moment. "You mentioned you hate rock chips; this is exactly where our PPF would protect the paint."
  • The Proposal & Close: After the assessment, present a tailored solution. "Based on what we've discussed, it sounds like the right solution is [Package]. Does this feel like the answer for you and your vehicle?"

Section 4: Advanced Questioning Techniques

How to Handle "Just Give Me a Price"

This is a request to build value. Acknowledge their request, then pivot back to diagnosis.

Script: "I can absolutely get you a price. To make sure it's accurate and that I'm recommending the right solution for you, could you first tell me a little bit about what you're hoping to protect your car from?"

How to Extract Direct Responses

Use simple, direct questions to confirm the customer's needs and get "yes" responses that build momentum towards the sale.

  • For PPF: "So, you'd agree that protecting your new car's paint from rock chips is a priority, right?"
  • For Ceramic Coating: "Would you like to make your car much easier to clean and keep it looking glossy all the time?"
  • For Ceramic Tint: "Are you interested in the best possible heat rejection to keep your family comfortable in the summer?"
