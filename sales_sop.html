Sales SOPs & Checklists

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) are essential for delivering a consistent, high-quality customer experience. This framework outlines the process for in-person consultations, combining our NEPQ principles with practical, actionable steps.

SOP: In-Person Consultation Framework

Phase 1: The Warm Welcome & Discovery

The goal is to build rapport and uncover the client's needs before you even get to the vehicle. This is where you connect with the client on a personal level.

  • Welcome: Start with a warm greeting and thank them for coming in.
  • Qualify: Ask a broad, open-ended question to understand their motivation. "What brought you in today?"
  • Identify the "Why": Use NEPQ questions to get to the heart of their needs. "Is your primary goal to protect the paint, enhance the look, or make the car easier to clean?"
  • Listen Actively: Pay close attention to their answers. These details are your key selling points for the rest of the conversation.

Phase 2: The On-Site Vehicle Assessment (The Walk-Around)

This is where you transition from conversation to a hands-on, educational experience. The walk-around should be about educating and building value, not just selling.

  • Assess "As-Is" Condition: Begin the walk-around by politely pointing out any existing scratches, rock chips, or swirls. This builds a case for the need for your services.
  • Educate with the Vehicle: Use the physical car as a teaching tool. Explain how a ceramic coating would protect against future water spots or how PPF would prevent rock chips.
  • Use Physical Samples: Bring out samples of your ceramic coating, PPF, or window tint film. Let the customer feel the product's quality.
  • Offer a Tailored Solution: Based on your conversation, recommend a specific package that addresses their needs.

Phase 3: The Proposal & Close

Bring the conversation back to the office to present the final quote and secure the booking.

  • Review the Proposal: Walk the client through the itemized quote, explaining the value of each service.
  • Address Objections: Be prepared to handle common objections like price or timing.
  • Encourage Commitment: Use your committing questions to gently guide the client toward a decision. "Based on what we've discussed, does this feel like the right solution for you and your vehicle?"
  • Confirm Next Steps: Once they agree, take the deposit, schedule the appointment, and discuss your referral program.
