Sales SOP & Checklists

This is the master checklist for the entire customer journey. Following these Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) ensures a consistent, professional, and high-quality experience for every client.

Phase 1: Inquiry & Follow-Up

The goal is to provide a rapid, professional response to every inquiry and convert them into a scheduled consultation.

Follow-Up Cadence Flowchart

Initial Inquiry → Day 1-5: Daily Follow-Up (Call, Voicemail, Text) → Weeks 2-5: Weekly Follow-Up (Call, Voicemail) → Ongoing: Monthly Follow-Up (Call or Text)

  • All inquiries are managed in GoHighLevel and must be logged in the customer follow-up log.
  • The standard procedure is to **always call first, then immediately send a text.**

Phase 2: Vehicle Drop-Off & Onboarding

This is where we build trust, diagnose the vehicle, and set clear expectations.

Onboarding Checklist

  • Create Invoice in Urable: Enter all customer and vehicle information (VIN, plate, mileage) while with the client.
  • Perform Digital Inspection:
    Urable Digital Inspection Protocol

    A complete inspection is non-negotiable. Required photos include: all four corners of the vehicle, front and rear, odometer showing mileage, and clear close-ups of any pre-existing damage (swirls, scratches, curb rash, interior stains).

  • Conduct Vehicle Walk-Around: Physically point out damages to the customer. This is your opportunity to diagnose their needs using NEPQ skills.
  • Use In-Shop Displays: Use the tint heat lamp, ceramic-coated hood, and PPF displays to demonstrate the real-world benefits of our services and plant seeds for upsells.

    Resource Link

    Refer to the Selling Window Tint, Selling PPF, and Selling Ceramic Coatings guides for key talking points.

  • Set Final Expectations: Review the scope of work, expected outcomes, and pick-up time. Answer any questions.
  • Secure Approval & Deposit: Obtain a customer signature for the work approval in Urable and take a 50% deposit.

Phase 3: During Service Communication

Maintain communication and transparency while the vehicle is in our care.

Communication Protocol

  • Monitor technician progress and photos in Urable.
  • Use compelling before/after photos to provide the customer with a progress update.
  • If an upsell opportunity arises (e.g., heavy swirls are discovered that need more correction), use the photos to get customer approval for additional work.

Phase 4: Vehicle Pick-Up & Handoff

The final handoff is our last chance to make a great impression and build a long-term customer relationship.

Handoff Checklist

  • Walk the customer around the vehicle, showcasing the completed work.
  • Obtain signature on the "Work Completed" approval form.
  • **Educate on Maintenance:** Explain proper aftercare. Provide any warranty booklets (for coatings/film) and the official CARPRO maintenance guide.
  • **Sell Maintenance Products:** Recommend the TDS Washbox or other essential maintenance items.
  • **Schedule Next Appointment:** Proactively offer to book their first maintenance wash.
  • **Take Final Payment.**
  • **Reinforce Referral Program:** Hand the customer 3 physical referral cards. **Instruct them: "Be sure to write your name on the back of these before you give them out so you get your service credit."**
  • **Ask for a Review:** Inform them that an automated link will be sent from Urable in 24 hours and that we would greatly appreciate their feedback with photos.

Handoff Script Starters

For Selling Products: "To keep this new coating looking its best, the two most important things are a pH-neutral soap and a clean drying towel. Our maintenance kit right here has everything you need to get started."

For Scheduling Next Service: "The first wash is the most important for a new coating. While you're here, let's get your first maintenance wash on the calendar for about two weeks from now."
