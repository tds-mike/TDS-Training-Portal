Urable: Our Digital Hub

Urable is the central nervous system of our shop. It's how we manage the entire customer journey, from the initial inspection to the final invoice and review request. Mastering this tool is essential for both the sales and technical teams to ensure a smooth, professional, and transparent process for every client.

Phase 1 (Sales): Vehicle Intake & Onboarding

The onboarding process in Urable is our first opportunity to build trust and document the vehicle's condition accurately.

Intake Checklist

  • Create New Invoice: While the customer is present, create a new invoice. Accurately enter all customer details and vehicle information, including the VIN, license plate, and current mileage.
  • Perform the Digital Inspection: This is a non-negotiable step. Use the Urable app on a tablet to take and upload photos directly to the "Inspection" tab.
    Required Inspection Photos:
    • All four corners of the vehicle.
    • Front and rear profiles.
    • A clear photo of the odometer showing mileage.
    • Crucially: Close-up, well-lit photos of any pre-existing damage. This includes swirls, scratches, rock chips, curb rash on wheels, and interior stains. Over-documenting protects the shop.
  • Build the Work Order: Add the specific services the customer has agreed to, including any upsells discussed during the walk-around.
  • Secure Approval & Deposit: Once the work order is complete, have the customer sign for approval directly in the app. Process the 50% deposit through the Urable payment system.

Phase 2 (Technician): In-Service Documentation

For technicians, Urable is the logbook for the job, showcasing your craftsmanship and communicating progress.

Technician Protocol

  • Review the Work Order: Before starting, open the job in Urable and thoroughly review the scope of work and any notes from the sales team. Check the inspection photos to understand the vehicle's starting condition.
  • Upload Progress Photos: Throughout the service, take compelling photos and upload them to the "Technician Photos" section.

    What Makes a Great Progress Photo?

    The best photos are 50/50 shots (showing a clear before/after line) and dramatic transformations (e.g., a heavily soiled interior seat half-cleaned, or a paint panel half-corrected). These are powerful marketing assets and prove the value of our work to the client.

  • Document Issues: If you discover a new issue (e.g., failing clear coat under a sticker), take a photo, upload it immediately, and add a note for the Shop Manager. This creates a time-stamped record.

Phase 3 (Sales): Handoff & Closing

Urable streamlines the final steps of the customer journey, ensuring a professional close-out and encouraging future business.

Handoff Checklist

  • Showcase the Work: Use the technician's photos in Urable on a tablet to walk the customer through the transformation, reinforcing the value of the service they received.
  • Obtain Final Approval: Have the customer sign the "Work Completed" approval form within the invoice.
  • Process Final Payment: Take the remaining payment through the Urable system.
  • Automated Review Request: Inform the customer, "In about 24 hours, our system will automatically send you a link to leave a review. We'd be grateful if you'd share your experience and some photos of the finished result!"
