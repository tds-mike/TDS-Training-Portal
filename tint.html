The Art of Window Tinting

Module 1: Science, Strategy & Law

Why We Tint: More Than Just Looks

Window tinting is a high-skill service that provides tangible benefits beyond aesthetics. Our films provide UV Protection (blocking 99%+ of harmful rays), Heat Rejection (keeping the cabin cooler), Glare Reduction (for safer driving), and Privacy.

Understanding Film Types

  • Carbon Film: A mid-tier option that won't fade and offers moderate heat rejection.
  • Ceramic/IR Film: The premium choice. Contains ceramic nanoparticles that block infrared radiation (heat) without needing to be dark. Offers the highest heat rejection and won't interfere with electronic signals. Note: Ceramic film is thicker and requires more heat and time to shrink properly.

VLT & Connecticut Tint Law

VLT (Visible Light Transmittance) is the percentage of light the film allows through. Lower numbers are darker.

  • Sedans: Fronts & Rears must be 35% or lighter.
  • SUVs/Trucks: Fronts must be 35% or lighter. Rears can be any darkness.
  • Windshield: No film permitted below the AS-1 line.

Module 2: Our Tinting Arsenal

Contamination is the Enemy

Window tinting is a "clean room" operation. A single speck of dust or hair can ruin an entire piece of film. Success is determined before the film even touches the glass. Minimize air movement, work in a clean bay, and be mindful of contamination at all times.

Tools & Materials

  • Squeegees: Fusion Turbo, Orange Crush, Yellow Flat Out, Side Swiper, Bulldozer
  • Hard Cards: Gold Hard Card (for shrinking), Corner Cards (Little Foot, Shank, etc.)
  • Prep Tools: Cleaning Scrub Pads, Flat Razor Blade, 30° Olfa Blade Knife
  • Solutions: Slip Solution (1oz/gallon), Cleaning Solution
  • Heat Gun: Wagner heat gun or equivalent

Procedure: Surgical Glass Prep

The mantra is: "Spray it, Scrub it, Squeegee it." Any missed debris will be trapped permanently.

Side Windows

Watch: Side Window Glass Prep

[Video Tutorial Placeholder]

Protect the interior with towels. Lower the window 1-2 inches. Spray, scrub, and squeegee the top edge clean, then wipe the very top of the glass with a lint-free towel until it's perfect. Raise the window, clean the main surface (using a razor on older vehicles), and use a corner card to flush water and debris from the side and bottom seals.

Front Windshield

Place a "soak rope" at the base of the dash to absorb water. Meticulously clean the entire surface with a scrub pad and squeegee. Pay special attention to the corners and edges, flushing out all debris.

Procedure: Final Application & Installation Methods

Method 1: Seal Removal (Primary)

Our preferred method for the cleanest install. By removing the side window seal, you can slide the film all the way to the bottom of the glass, ensuring no peeling edges.

Watch: Seal Removal Installation

[Video Tutorial Placeholder]

  1. Generously spray the clean glass with slip solution.
  2. Peel the liner and spray the film's adhesive side.
  3. Float the film onto the glass, leaving a micro-edge at the top.
  4. Squeegee from the center outwards to lock the film and remove all water.
Method 2: Two-Stage (Seals Intact)

Used when a seal cannot be removed. Requires more skill to avoid contamination.

Watch: Two-Stage Installation

[Video Tutorial Placeholder]

  1. Apply the film to the top half of the window only and squeegee it out.
  2. Roll the window up. Carefully peel back the bottom half of the film.
  3. Spray slip solution behind the film and on the bottom half of the glass.
  4. Tuck the bottom edge of the film below the seal and squeegee out the remaining water.

Module 4: Quality Control & Handoff

Troubleshooting: The "Check Your Work" Protocol

The TDS Standard: When in doubt, redo it.

Before delivery, inspect your work from inside and outside with a strong light. Our reputation is built on quality. Anything less than your best work is unacceptable.

  • Debris/Dirt Bubbles: More than a few tiny, isolated specks of dust is unacceptable and must be replaced.
  • Creases or Scratches: Any visible crease or scratch in the film is permanent damage and must be replaced.
  • Edge Gaps & Lift Lines: Check for even gaps. Any edge that is not lying perfectly flat must be fixed or the piece must be replaced.

The Customer Handoff Script

Educate the client on the critical curing process to prevent comebacks.

The Curing Process: "Your new tint is still in a curing phase. You may notice some small water pockets or a slightly hazy look—this is normal and will disappear as the moisture evaporates through the film over the next few days."

CRITICAL Aftercare Instructions: "The most important thing is to please do not roll down the windows for at least 3 to 5 days. This gives the adhesive time to fully bond. For cleaning, please use an ammonia-free glass cleaner and a soft microfiber towel."
