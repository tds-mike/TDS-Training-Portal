Safety is the foundation of professionalism. Always know your chemicals and how to handle them.

  • Eye Contact: Immediately flush with water. An eye rinse station and first-aid supplies are located in the main bathroom.

The Science of Cleaning

Understanding the fundamental types of chemicals we use is crucial for safety and effectiveness. Each chemical is a specialized tool designed for a specific job.

Key Chemical Components

  • Surfactants: Found in car wash soaps, they reduce surface tension to lift and emulsify dirt and grease.
  • Solvents: Dissolve sticky and oily residues like tar, grease, and adhesives.
  • Abrasives: Fine particles in polishes/compounds that physically remove imperfections from paintwork.
  • Protectants: Waxes, sealants, and coatings that provide a protective barrier against UV rays and contaminants.

Understanding the pH Scale (0-14)

The pH scale is critical for choosing the right chemical. Using the wrong pH can damage surfaces.

Acidic (pH Below 7)

Effective at removing mineral deposits, water spots, and rust. (e.g., CARPRO Descale)

Neutral (pH 7)

Gentle and safe for regular maintenance washes. (e.g., CARPRO Reset)

Alkaline/Basic (pH Above 7)

Excellent at cutting through grease, oils, and heavy organic grime. (e.g., CARPRO Lift)

Shop Protocols & Best Practices

Chemical Handling & Storage

  • Label Everything: Any chemical transferred into a secondary container (like a spray bottle) MUST be clearly labeled with the chemical name and its dilution ratio.
  • Proper Storage: Always return chemicals to their designated cabinets with caps sealed tightly. Flammable liquids must be kept in the fire-rated safety cabinet.
  • Check for Leaks: Regularly inspect bottles and spray heads for any leaks or damage.

Tool & Applicator Management

One Tool, One Purpose

To prevent cross-contamination, towels, pads, and brushes must be dedicated. A towel used for sealant should never touch a car being prepped for coating. Dedicate specific applicators for specific jobs (e.g., separate pads for compounding, polishing, and waxing).

Shop Environment & Personal Habits

Work Clean, Work Safe

  • Ventilation: Ensure good airflow, especially when using solvents (TarX, Eraser) or applying ceramic coatings. Open bay doors when possible.
  • Product Conservation: Spray chemicals onto your towel or applicator, not the surface. This prevents wasteful overspray and gives you more control.
  • Waste Disposal: Properly dispose of used towels. Towels soaked with solvents or coatings should be placed in designated, covered fire-safe trash cans.
